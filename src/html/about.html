Who Am I?

I'm just a curious mind, trying to make sense of the world one line of code at a time. This blog started as a place for me to document my journey into web development, but it quickly became much more. Here, I share my thoughts, lessons learned, and my attempts to tackle problems, break things, and rebuild them in the process.

As a self-taught developer, this space is as much about learning from my mistakes as it is about celebrating small victories along the way. My journey has been one of constant learning, and I believe that the best way to learn is by actually building things — messing up, debugging, and iterating as you go.

What You’ll Find Here

  • Practical guides on web development, no fluff, just what works
  • Personal stories about tackling complex problems and learning from them
  • My take on building things from scratch, and the struggles (and triumphs) that come with it
  • Exploring core web technologies like HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and more, with simple examples

Why This Blog Exists

I didn’t start this blog with the goal of becoming a web development expert, but rather to share my own experience as I learn and grow in this field. If my posts help even one person avoid the same mistakes I made or inspire them to dive deeper into coding, then I’ve done my job.

This blog isn’t about having all the answers; it’s about documenting progress and encouraging others to start, learn, and build. We’re all learning together, and that’s what makes this journey exciting.

Quick Facts

  • Built using pure HTML, CSS, and JavaScript (no frameworks here!)
  • Every post is a reflection of real-world problems and solutions
  • Focused on practical learning and real-life application
